SCIENCE POWERED BY CURIOSITY

The Bhamla Lab is a group of biophysicists and bioengineers who focus on cultivating an often overlooked attribute in science: curiosity.

From worm blobs to cyborg cells to $1 hearing aids, our work seeks to understand how organisms do amazing things, the physics behind life’s remarkable materials, and how we’re able to use simple concepts to build frugal technology for global health.

Acrobatic Springtails Inspire New Robotics

We study the mechanics of springtails, which leap in the water to avoid predators. We learned how the tiny hexapods control their jump, self-right in midair, and land on their feet in the blink of an eye. We used the findings to build penny-sized jumping robots.

We are looking for curiousity-driven researchers, from undergraduates to postdocs, to join our team. Our research interests cover a wide range of topics: frugal tools for science and global health, extreme and ultra-fast organismic biophysics, and soft-matter physics. To apply, please send a copy of recent CV, a brief summary of your projects, and a short statement of your research interests.
